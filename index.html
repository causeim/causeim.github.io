A Coaxial Spherical Parallel Mechanism for Agile Life-sized Robotics

Jaeho Noh

Manuscript in preparation · 2026

I present a coaxial spherical parallel mechanism that concentrates four actuators at the pelvis while distributing load equally across all of them. The resulting limb achieves four times the continuous force capacity of a single-actuator baseline, reduces per-gear load to one quarter, and — via a Wöhler-style life model — extends gear life by 64×. I show the mechanism's applicability to bipedal locomotion and to high-payload manipulation within a single kinematic family.
