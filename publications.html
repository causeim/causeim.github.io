A Coaxial Spherical Parallel Mechanism for Agile Life-sized Robotics

Jaeho Noh

Manuscript in preparation · target venue TBD · 2026

This paper introduces a Coaxial Spherical Parallel Mechanism (Coaxial SPM) designed for life-sized legged platforms. The mechanism places four actuators at the pelvis and arranges their outputs coaxially so that vertical, radial, and lateral end-effector loads are distributed equally across every actuator. I derive the analytic kinematics, quantify the 4× effective strength gain and the 64× gear-life extension that follow from the load-sharing model, and validate the design on a bipedal platform with a target body mass of roughly 35 kg. I further show that the same kinematic family applies to heavy-payload manipulation — a 40 kg workspace-wide payload on the same limb — illustrating that one well-chosen mechanism can unify agile locomotion and industrial-scale manipulation.
