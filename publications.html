SFM

Kinematic and Load-Distribution Analysis of a 4-DOF Parallel Mechanism Built from Paired Coaxial Spherical Five-bar Sets

Jaeho Noh

Manuscript · target venue TBD · 2026

This paper proposes a Spatial Five-bar Mechanism (SFM): a 4-DOF parallel mechanism built from two Coaxial and Symmetric 5R Spherical Parallel Mechanism (CoSy 5R-SPM) sets that share a common rotation axis. Together with the mechanism, I introduce a kinematic-performance framework tailored to its intended use, formalizing three design goals — balanced vertical load sharing, a wide range of motion, and low end-effector impedance — and three local indices to quantify them: the Local Conditioning Index (LCI), the Torque Amplification Ratio (TAR), and a newly proposed Load Distribution Index (LDI). The SFM's architecture is formalized as the radial vector sum of two CoSy 5R-SPM outputs, which lets the forward / inverse kinematics and the Jacobian be derived in closed form as the sum of two independent 2-DOF analyses. Evaluating LCI, TAR, and LDI across the workspace shows that the SFM (i) reaches roughly 92% of its enclosing sphere with in-principle unbounded coaxial rotation, (ii) shares end-effector loads broadly evenly among its four actuators, and (iii) structurally eliminates cascaded reflected rotor inertia at the end-effector. The mechanism is compared against a matched 4-DOF serial manipulator and applied to two case studies — an industrial robotic arm and a bipedal robot leg.
